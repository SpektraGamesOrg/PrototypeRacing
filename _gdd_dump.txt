4.  Core  Design  Pillars  
Three  pillars.  Every  gameplay  feature  must  serve  at  least  one.  Any  feature  that  does  not  serve  any  of  these  is  cut.

5.  Core  Loop  
5.1  First-session  flow  
Targeted  90-second  first-session  experience  before  any  ad  interruption:  •  0-3s:  Splash  logo  →  direct  to  garage  (no  splash  screen  longer  than  2  seconds).  •  3-5s:  Garage  shows  starter  car  (Spectre  GT8).  Large  DRIVE  button,  pulsing.  •  5-8s:  Player  taps  DRIVE.  1-second  fade  to  gameplay.  Car  spawns  on  highway  with  traffic  flowing.  •  8-60s:  Free  driving.  First  skill  event  (a  drift  or  a  jump)  triggers  a  big  on-screen  'BONUS:  500  CASH'  popup  
with
 
confetti
 
particles.
 
Dopamine
 
hit
 
delivered.
 •  60-120s:  Player  discovers  ramps,  collectibles,  destructibles  through  environmental  cues  (glowing  coins,  
bright-colored
 
ramps,
 
breakable
 
crates
 
on
 
the
 
edge
 
of
 
the
 
road).
 •  120s:  First  interstitial.  Game  pauses,  ad  plays,  return  to  gameplay  exactly  where  they  left  off.  •  120s+:  Steady-state  session  loop  (below).  
5.2  Steady-state  session  loop  
Per  session  after  session  1:  •  App  open  →  App  Open  Ad  (see  18.2)  →  Garage  (1-2  sec)  •  Tap  DRIVE  →  gameplay  starts  •  Player  drives,  earns  cash  from  skill  events  +  pickups  +  destructibles  •  Every  ~120s  of  active  gameplay:  interstitial  ad  •  Car  takes  damage  over  time  →  at  0  HP,  rewarded  ad  to  repair  (or  30s  wait)  •  Nitro  button:  rewarded  ad  for  full  nitro  tank  refill  +  bonus  power  (see  5.3)  •  Player  returns  to  menu  →  interstitial  ad  on  exit  to  garage  •  In  garage,  player  can:  buy  new  car  (soft  currency),  unlock  car  (rewarded  ads),  swap  paint,  swap  rims,  or  
re-enter
 
gameplay

6.  Driving  Model  and  Physics  
6.1  Physics  philosophy  
Arcade,  not  simulation.  We  tune  for  ONE  thing:  the  car  should  feel  like  a  toy  the  player  is  throwing  around.  
Specifically:
 •  Acceleration:  0-100  km/h  in  2-4  seconds  depending  on  car  tier.  Unrealistically  fast.  This  matters  because  
session
 
length
 
is
 
short
 
and
 
top
 
speed
 
has
 
to
 
be
 
achievable
 
inside
 
10
 
seconds
 
on
 
any
 
straight.
 •  Top  speed  band:  220-360  km/h  across  the  roster.  Every  car  hits  200+.  The  speed-earning  threshold  (150  
km/h,
 
see
 
7.1)
 
must
 
be
 
easily
 
reachable.
 •  Grip:  High  lateral  grip  by  default.  Loss  of  grip  only  happens  when  (a)  drift  toggle  is  ON  and  player  turns  
sharply,
 
or
 
(b)
 
handbrake
 
is

Drift
 
should
 
feel
 
like
 
a
 
choice,
 
not
 
an
 
accident.
 •  Drift  angle  cap:  ~55  degrees  yaw  offset.  Bigger  than  real,  smaller  than  cartoony.  Satisfying  to  hold.  •  Air  control:  Yes.  During  jumps,  player  can  nose  up/down  with  the  tilt  arrows  or  swipe.  Car  rotates  slowly  to  
allow
 
for
 
stunts
 
(barrel

bonus

 •  Landing:  Generous.  Cars  that  land  off-angle  auto-correct  over  0.5  seconds.  A  hard  landing  costs  5-15  HP  
but

end

Missed

a
 
failure.
